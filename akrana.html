Our core flovour :

♦ Cocolates


The texture adds to the flavor experience, as the rich, creamy base coats the tongue, intensifying the chocolate flavor with each bite. Whether it’s the familiar sweetness of milk chocolate or the intense depth of dark chocolate, the flavor is both comforting and satisfying, making it a timeless favorite.Optional mix-ins, like chocolate chunks, fudge ribbons, or nuts, can add additional textures and bursts of chocolate flavor, making each scoop a unique experience.



♦ Vanilla


Vanilla ice cream is often considered a perfect canvas, its simplicity allowing it to pair beautifully with a wide range of toppings and mix-ins, from chocolate sauce and fruits to nuts or caramel swirls. Its elegant simplicity makes it a favorite for those who appreciate a refined, yet comforting flavor that can be enjoyed on its own or as a complement to other desserts.



♦ Strawberry


strawberry ice cream features small chunks or swirls of real fruit or strawberry puree, adding texture and intensifying the flavor with little bursts of fresh fruit. It’s a perfect balance of fruity, creamy, and sweet, making it a universally loved treat that’s both comforting and refreshing. Whether served on its own or paired with other desserts like chocolate cake or shortcake, strawberry ice cream is always a nostalgic and satisfying indulgence.

☎ Delivery Method :

    ⚹ 1. In-House Delivery (Local Delivery) :

  • This is when your business handles the delivery using your own drivers or delivery team. You can offer this service for local customers, typically within a specific radius around your ice cream shop (e.g., 5–10 miles). Delivery Time Slots: Allow customers to select a preferred time window for delivery, such as within 1-2 hours or a specific delivery time (e.g., between 2-4 PM).

  • Delivery Charges: You can set a flat fee for local delivery or a variable fee based on distance from your store. Real-Time Tracking: If possible, integrate tracking for customers to monitor the status of their delivery, such as "order preparing," "out for delivery," and "delivered."

    •

    ⚹ 2. Third-Party Delivery Services (Zomato) :

  • Services like UberEats, DoorDash, or Grubhub: Partnering with a third-party delivery platform allows you to leverage their network for delivering orders to customers. These platforms already have delivery systems in place, making it easier for you. Integration with Your Website: If you partner with third-party services, you can integrate their order system directly into your website, so customers can choose to have their orders delivered via these platforms.

  • Fees & Commissions: Be aware that third-party services charge commission fees, typically ranging from 15-30% of the order total. Pickup (Self-Pickup)

  • Pickup Option: Offer customers the ability to pick up their order from your shop. This is often faster and doesn't involve delivery charges. Pickup Timing: Allow customers to choose when they want to pick up their order, either right away or at a later time. Pickup Instructions: Provide clear instructions for how to collect the order (e.g., curbside pickup, counter pickup, etc.).

    ⚹ 3. Delivery Radius & Restrictions :

  • Limitations: Clearly define the delivery area (e.g., only within a 5-mile radius). You may want to limit delivery based on location or distance to keep it manageable.

  • Availability: If you can only deliver at certain times (e.g., during business hours), make this clear on the website.

₹ Payment Methods:


    ⚹ 1. Credit/Debit Card Payment :

  • Secure Payment Gateway: Integrate a reliable, secure payment system (like Stripe, Square, or PayPal) to process credit and debit card payments. Multiple Card Types: Accept popular credit and debit card brands (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, etc.).

  • Tokenization: Use tokenization technology for security to protect customer data during the transaction. Digital Wallets & Mobile Payments

    ⚹ 2. Apple Pay & Google Pay :

  • Allow customers to make payments easily through mobile wallets like Apple Pay and Google Pay.

  • PayPal: Offer PayPal as an option for customers who prefer to use their PayPal balance or linked bank accounts.

  • Other Wallets: If popular in your region, consider adding additional wallet options like Samsung Pay, Venmo, etc.

    ⚹ 3. Cash on Delivery (COD) :

  • Some customers may prefer to pay with cash upon receiving their order. This is more common for local deliveries.

  • COD Limitations: Make sure to clarify any restrictions (e.g., cash payment might only be available within a certain radius or for small orders).

  • Payment Confirmation: Ensure clear communication on the order page regarding payment options and COD eligibility.
